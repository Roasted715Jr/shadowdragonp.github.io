Life as a Game Developer

How I started developing games is a rather interesting story.

In 8th grade, I was in two state-wide competitions. The first one was BPA, and the second was TSA. In BPA, one of the events was to design a game from scratch with the theme of "driving." Back then, I had zero video game development experience, so I figured that I might as well learn it. Our team first chose Pygame, a compiler for games written in Python.

Bad idea.

Our "game" was bad. Really bad. By the time it was due, it was unfinished, full of bugs, and you couldn't do much with it. This was primarily because of how limited Python is, but I knew that I did not want to continue down the path of Python. Meanwhile, TSA began and video game design was also an event. In TSA, contestants were allowed to use engines instead of coding games from scratch. We chose Unity3D, meaning I had to learn some C#.

Again, our game was unfinished, but I knew that Unity3D was what I had been looking for. It met all of my needs. It was user-friendly, fairly easy to understand, and was capable of producing high-quality games. Since then, I have been developing games in Unity3D.
